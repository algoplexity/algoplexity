## **Mesoscopic Algorithmic Theory of Collective Intelligence**

### **Pitch Summary**

We propose a **cybernetic, algorithmic theory of collective intelligence**: collective intelligence is not a property of individuals or outputs, but a **relational property of coordinated systems**, measurable via the **compressibility of observed interactions**. Using a bounded observer and neural algorithmic complexity estimators, we define a **coordination energy** that enables **real-time observation, visualization, and steering** of collective dynamics across biological, social, and artificial systems.

---

### **Core Hypothesis**

Let ($$A_1, …, A_n$$) be agents. A bounded observer captures partial interaction trajectories ($$O(A_i)$$). Using neural algorithmic complexity estimators (Neural BDM), define **coordination energy**:

$$
E = \sum_{i=1}^{n} K_{\text{est}}(O(A_i)) - K_{\text{est}}(O(A_1, …, A_n))
$$

**Principle:**

> Lower ($E$) → higher coordination (effective collective intelligence)
> 
> Higher ($E$) → fragmentation, redundancy, or incoherence

---

### **Observer-Dependence**

* Observations ($$O(A_i)$$) are defined by sensors, sampling rate, and accessible features.
* Collective intelligence is thus **relative to what the system can perceive**, making the measure **operational in real-world CPS settings**.

---

### **Dynamics & Control**

* ($$E(t)$$) is dynamic; low-energy regimes correspond to coherent coordination.
* Energy spikes indicate misalignment, instability, or inefficient coordination.
* Cybernetic control loops can **steer the system to minimize ($$E$$)**, maintaining system viability and ethical operation.

---

### **Generality Across Systems**

| System Type | Agents | Observable Dynamics                  |
| ----------- | ------ | ------------------------------------ |
| Flocks      | Birds  | Trajectories, velocities             |
| Swarms      | Ants   | Movement patterns, pheromones        |
| Schools     | Fish   | Positions, alignment                 |
| Human Teams | People | Speech, movement, decision sequences |
| AI Agents   | Models | Messages, embeddings                 |

All share **distributed information exchange under constraints**, making the theory **substrate-agnostic**.

---

### **Implications**

* **Measurement:** Coordination energy is a quantifiable state variable for collective intelligence.
* **Visualization:** Trajectories of ($$E(t)$$) reveal system health and phase transitions.
* **Control & Ethics:** Interventions can maintain low-energy regimes, identifying and mitigating unsafe or undesired behavior.

---

### **One-Line Synthesis**

> **Collective intelligence emerges when multiple agents produce behavior that admits a compressible joint description relative to observer-accessible trajectories.**
