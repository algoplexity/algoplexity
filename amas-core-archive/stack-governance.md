# invariant-structure-core / meta / stack-governance

## AMAS Governance Constraints for the Invariant Structure Stack

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the **global admissibility constraints governing AMAS-core structure and its derived layers**.

It does not define:

* ontology
* theory
* measurement
* computation
* dynamics

It defines:

> conditions under which any such definitions remain internally consistent and non-degenerate.

Governance operates at the level of **validity constraints**, not structural construction.

---

## 2. Governance Scope

The system is organized into five coupled domains:

* Ontology (what is admissible)
* Theory (what relations are admissible)
* Measurement (what is observable under bounded projection)
* Computation (what transformations are admissible)
* Dynamics (what evolutions are admissible)

Governance applies across all domains symmetrically.

It does not privilege any domain as control authority.

---

## 3. Layer Non-Interference

Each domain is semantically isolated.

Specifically:

* Ontology cannot encode measurement procedures
* Theory cannot define computational implementations
* Measurement cannot redefine ontological primitives
* Computation cannot alter structural or observational definitions
* Dynamics cannot be modified by downstream processes

No domain is permitted to redefine the admissibility conditions of another.

---

## 4. Directionality Constraint

AMAS defines a strict **definition direction**, not a control hierarchy:

```
ontology → theory → measurement → computation → systems
```

and separately:

```
dynamics governs admissible evolution across all layers
```

Key constraint:

> Directionality applies to definitional dependency, not causal control.

No reverse definitional influence is permitted.

---

## 5. Epistemic Separation Principle

Three epistemic roles must remain distinct:

* Structure: what is defined as admissible
* Representation: what is observed under bounded projection
* Computation: what is transformable under rules

Collapse of these roles produces invalid system semantics.

---

## 6. Computation Non-Authority Constraint

Computational processes, estimators, and learning systems are **non-authoritative** with respect to AMAS definitions.

Specifically:

* computation may not define structural primitives
* computation may not redefine measurement mappings
* inference may not modify dynamics
* learning may not alter invariants

Computation is strictly an **internal transformation regime**, not a definitional layer.

---

## 7. Observer Constraint (AMAS-aligned)

All measurement is observer-relative:

[
x_t = \phi_{\mathcal{O}}(X_t)
]

However:

* observer dependence affects representation
* observer dependence does NOT affect structural admissibility

Therefore:

> structural equivalence is invariant across all admissible observers

Observers differ in resolution, not in ontology.

---

## 8. Valid Transformation Constraint

A transformation is valid only if it preserves:

* invariant structure classes (𝕀-consistency)
* admissibility under dynamics (𝔻-consistency)
* non-degeneracy of representation across observers (𝕄-consistency)

Invalid transformations include:

* those that collapse distinct admissible structures
* those that introduce unbounded or undefined evolution
* those that violate invariant preservation

---

## 9. Dynamics Constraint Clarification (AMAS critical)

Dynamics is not a control system.

It is:

> the constraint system defining admissible state evolution across all representations

Therefore:

* dynamics is not optimized
* dynamics is not learned
* dynamics is not inferred as a policy

It is a **fixed admissibility law over temporal evolution**.

---

## 10. Governance Principle Summary

The system is valid only if:

* domains remain semantically isolated
* computation remains non-authoritative
* observer dependence does not affect structural equivalence
* dynamics remains non-modifiable by downstream processes
* transformations preserve admissibility constraints

---

## 11. Role of Governance

Governance does not construct AMAS.

It enforces:

> global non-degeneracy conditions ensuring that AMAS remains a well-defined admissibility system under abstraction, projection, and computation.
