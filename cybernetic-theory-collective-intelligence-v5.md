# **Mesoscopic Cybernetic Theory of Collective Intelligence**

---

## **Abstract**

We propose a cybernetic, information-theoretic theory of collective intelligence grounded in **observer-dependent algorithmic compressibility**. Collective intelligence is defined as a relational property of multi-agent systems: a system is collectively intelligent when its joint behavior admits a low-complexity generative description relative to a bounded observer, while retaining non-trivial dynamics.

Crucially, intelligence arises through the **coordination of interacting components (“society of thoughts”)**, rather than isolated computation. This structure is not predefined but must be inferred from interaction.

This induces measurable quantities—**generative complexity** and **coordination energy**—that define a phase space of collective dynamics. The framework enables observation and regulation of coordination across biological, social, and artificial systems, including embodied AI.
