## **Abstract (AMAS-Admissible v2.1)**

Collective behavior in multi-agent systems is widely observed across biological, social, and artificial domains, yet remains difficult to characterize in a general and formally testable manner. Existing approaches typically rely on statistical, structural, or data-driven descriptions, which capture observable regularities but do not directly address the generative representations underlying such systems.

In this work, we introduce a framework based on **observer-relative, algorithmic representations** of multi-agent systems. The framework defines a set of numerical functionals derived from approximations to Kolmogorov complexity applied to joint and component-wise representations of system behavior. These include (i) aggregate difference functionals, (ii) temporal difference functionals, and (iii) perturbation-based functionals defined over system components.

We formulate relationships between these quantities that can be evaluated empirically within a **falsifiable experimental setting**. To support this, we provide a general protocol for generating artifacts, constructing representations, and applying deterministic transformations under bounded observers. The protocol is model-free, unsupervised, and applicable across synthetic and real-world multi-agent systems.

This work establishes a formal basis for analyzing multi-agent behavior using algorithmic descriptions, while explicitly separating representation, measurement, and evaluation. All interpretations of the defined quantities are treated as hypotheses to be tested, enabling systematic investigation under a constraint-based falsifiability framework.
