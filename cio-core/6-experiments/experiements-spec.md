# 📜 CIO — Experimental Validation Layer (v1.0 — LOCKED)

---

## 🧭 0. PURPOSE

Defines:

> how CIO is **validated, tested, and falsified** in practice

---

## 🔒 This layer is:

* executable
* empirical
* instantiation-based

---

## ❌ This layer does NOT:

* define theory
* define observables
* define structure
* define invariants

---

# 🧠 1. ROLE

Experiments perform:

---

## ✔ 1.1 Validation

Test whether:

* measurement functionals behave consistently
* invariants are preserved

---

## ✔ 1.2 Falsification

Attempt to break:

* invariants
* observer equivalence
* estimator consistency

---

## ✔ 1.3 Comparison

Evaluate:

* different estimators (BDM, Neural BDM, etc.)
* different observers
* different representations

---

---

# 🧭 2. INPUTS

Experiments may use:

* representations ( x_t )
* estimators ( C_i )
* observer configurations ( O )

---

# 🧭 3. OUTPUTS

Experiments produce:

* empirical observations
* estimator comparisons
* validation reports

---

## 🔒 Constraint

Outputs are:

> NOT part of CIO definition

---

---

# 🧠 4. VALID EXPERIMENT TYPES

---

## 4.1 Estimator comparison

Compare:

* BDM vs Neural BDM
* AID vs compression

---

## 4.2 Perturbation experiments

Apply:

* node removal
* masking
* structural disruption

---

## 4.3 Observer variation

Change:

* encoding
* resolution
* representation

---

## 4.4 Phase detection

Test:

* regime changes
* discontinuities

---

---

# 🧭 5. CRITICAL CONSTRAINTS

---

## ❌ Experiments must NOT:

* redefine 𝒦_O or 𝒡_O
* introduce new invariants
* depend on specific estimators as truth

---

---

# 🔒 FINAL STATEMENT

> Experiments validate CIO — they do not define it.
