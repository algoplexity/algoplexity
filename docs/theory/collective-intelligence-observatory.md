# Collective Intelligence Observatory
## Canonical Theory Note

## Purpose

This document records the canonical theory framing for the Collective Intelligence Observatory (CIO).

## Canonical Statement

Collective intelligence is the emergence of compressible interaction structure, produced through dynamical processes and measurable in real time.

## Key Terms

- **Collective intelligence**: group-level coordination emerging from interaction.
- **Interaction structure**: the topology of relationships or exchanges in a group.
- **Dynamics**: the way that structure forms, stabilises, and changes over time.
- **Compressibility**: the degree to which the group can be described as a coherent whole rather than as unrelated parts.
- **Regime**: a recognisable pattern of coordinated behaviour or instability.

## Theory Commitments

This theory assumes:

- coordination is observable in group-level structure
- structure and dynamics are inseparable at the level of explanation
- measurement must be able to distinguish meaningful coordination from superficial synchrony
- any implementation must remain falsifiable against observable group behaviour
